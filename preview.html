The 3D animations below were inspired by the Jollibee Journal Square worker's rally to fight wage theft. At the time, many of my friends were also losing their jobs due to layoffs stemming from market forces beyond their control. I reflect on these events from the perspective of a corporate fast food chain mascot, a stand-in for the commodified public personas we must all wear to get by. Who are we when forces beyond our control take our livelihoods and reputations away? What futures are possible when we decide to leave behind the excess sold to us as a panacea for our woes?

Jollibee on a Smoke Break at the End of the World (2024)

Coming off the high of rallying the Bee Jolli workers, the beloved mascot becomes overwhelmed with grief, ennui, and anxiety. Having been fired from his job as the corporate mascot of Bee Jolli, he must find new meaning in a world fixated on hyperconsumerism and corporate greed. He ditches his beloved sports car and wallows at the end of the world.
