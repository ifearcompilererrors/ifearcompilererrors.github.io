ABOUT ME
"Sometimes you get a flash of what you look like to other people" - Zadie Smith, On Beauty



Angelique De Castro

Student. Tutor. Developer. California based.

I'm a class of 2016 Computer Science major at the University of California, San Diego. During the academic school year, I reside in La Jolla, California, and, during the summer, split my time between the Bay Area and Los Angeles.

I am currently interested in the development of technology that will help people utilize their resources to learn and achieve their goals. More specifically, I am passionate about innovating educational technology, as it has a growing influence and presence in global education.

What I've done to achieve this goal is work with the Foundation for Learning Equality on their KA Lite project as an intern. With FLE, I learned Python and Django to create an internal application with several other interns that would facilitate better communication amongst the members of the organization. Additionally, I work more closely with the KA Lite project by developing pieces needed to backup and restore software data.

I have also tutored for the Computer Science and Engineering Department's Object Oriented Design and Data Structures class under Christine Alvarado. On a weekly basis, I provided guidance for over 50 students with any help they needed with programming assignments and computing concepts.

Currently, I am a Designer and Developer for a web application for the SenseCam project at UCSD's Calit2.

Misc: I enjoy reading, writing, playing the guitar, drawing, working out, cooking, and caring for my succulents when I'm not coding.
