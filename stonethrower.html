Datu Lapu Lapu the Stone-thrower

This piece is a monument to the ongoing struggle for liberation from Palestine to the Philippines to Congo to Haiti to Sudan. It is inspired by Fadwa Tuqan's poem The Martyrs of the Intifada about young stone-throwers:

They died standing, blazing on the road
Shining like stars, their lips pressed to the lips of life
They stood up in the face of death
Then disappeared like the sun.

Resistance is not inherently peaceful. The name Datu Lapu Lapu was of the first native in what is now known as the Philippines to have rebelled against the Spaniards. He is rumored to have killed Magellan himself. There is a grouper fish named after him that fueled my most cherished memories of my parents' homeland.

Proceeds from the sale of this work will go toward helping Mohammed Elboji's family evacuate Gaza.
